🧮 The Mechanism

When a Latin pair collapses to “always no-switch”:

F1switch = 0  •  F1no-switch = 1

→ per-pair macro-F1 = 0.5 exactly (drags Mean F1 down)
→ but globally the no-switch class is now perfectly classified on those tokens (inflates Macro F1).

🎯 What This Diagnoses

Token-level Macro F1 rewards majority-class collapse. It cannot see that 4 of 10 pairs are broken because it pools every token into one bucket where CJK predictions dominate.

📐 Why Mean F1 + σ Win

Per-pair averaging penalizes unevenness across languages. The 0.126 Mean–Macro gap is the collapse. No other aggregate exposes it this cleanly.

💡

Methodological takeaway: Reporting Macro F1 alone would hide a representational collapse under a superficially improving 0.788 aggregate. Mean Anticipatory F1 and σuniversality are the correct metrics for cross-lingual code-switching evaluation — and Group E is the evidence.
